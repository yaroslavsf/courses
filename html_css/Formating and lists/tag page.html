TAG
[tæg]
noun
  1. a label attached to someone or something for the purpose of identification or to give other information.
    ■ an electronic device that can be attached to someone or something for monitoring purposes, e.g. to track offenders under house arrest or to deter shoplifters
    ■ a nickname or description popularly given to someone or something he lived up to his tag as the team's saviour
    Syn: tassel,label,ticket,mark.
  2. a small piece or part that is attached to a main body ■ a ragged lock of wool on a sheep ■ the tip of an animal's tail when it is distinctively coloured
verb
  1. attach a label to mothers suspected that their babies had been wrongly tagged during an alarm at the hospital
    to put a tag on smth. — прикрепить этикетку на что-л.
  2. attach an electronic tag to: [as noun] (tagging)the tagging of remand prisoners
    прикреплять ярлык, этикетку; маркировать
    He was tagged as a quitter. — За ним закрепилась слава лодыря и прогульщика.
